# Problem 
Assuming that a class of n students is assigned m concert tickets $(m<n)$.
The students decide to allocate tickets by lucky draw.
If you are $k$-th person to draw, but now it is known that for the first $k-1$ people,
no concert tickets have been drawn.
Then what is the probability for you to get a concert ticket?
