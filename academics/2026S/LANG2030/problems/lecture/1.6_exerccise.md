
Some may argue that the inaction of the engineers was due to the TEPCO management’s decision not to retrofit the older units because of the high expenditure associated with the upgrading process. However, this should not remove entirely the liability of the engineers, as they were obliged to express their honest opinions to their employers about the possible repercussions within their area of competence (WFEO articles 2.1, 4.3 in [11]). They should have explained to the management team the complications and threats that the flaw presented to the reactors. Admittedly it was a challenging situation that they operated in, and their obligations to the employers and their self-interest could have weighed heavily in their consideration, but they should also be aware that the interest of the public was paramount.
