# speed learning algorithm
- this is my personal speed learning algorithm
  - fully based on intuition
  - to be refined based on future experience
- ## motivation: 
  - act as a template 
    - template whenever it comes to studying
    - which speeds up the process 
      - since you would be doing the same template over and over again 
  - to not waste time on
    - wandering, doing nothing 
      - since you have a template to follow, it is unlikely to waste time on something irrelevant 
    - trying to understand concepts with barely any linkage to prior knowledge
      - this algorithm tells you when to stop thinking about it all yourself
- ## algorithm
  1. max 1 min read/listen to: 
     - get a clue
       - clue: 
         - anything that allows continuing the understanding process, that makes progress
       - at least a clue, any clue 
       - it could be simply presenting the knowledge in a different, easier way
         - usually by drawing, interpreting the concept in a more friendly way
     - be extremely focused and relax
       - to maximize the use of only 1 min
     - try your damn best to:
       - find linkage between the concept and prior knowledge that may support it
      
  2. max 3 mins to:
     - develop, from clue, to a basic and fundamental understanding of the concept (tiny a-ha moment) 
     - stop if:
       - reaches the above time limit
         - which implies: 
           -  no clue in 1 min 
           - too many clues within 3 mins (no direction)
         - then:
           - google it, 
           - llm it, 
           - ask friend 
           - ...
           - to develop the understanding, linkage with other knowledges 
  3. after getting fundamental understanding:
     - actively and immediately recall without re-reading
       - by writing down on a paper:
         - definition, 
         - motivation, 
         - meaning, 
         - linkages, 
         - ... 
         - using your own words (paraphrase)
  4. attempt one to a few problems with the [problem-solving algorithm](./speed_problem-solving_algo.md)
  5. refine notes:
     - from written on paper into digital notes
     - problem solved into digital solution 
     - which are your ultimate "crafts"
  6. attempt more problems with the [problem-solving algorithm](./speed_problem-solving_algo.md)
